# System Sizing & Selection

Determining the right hashrate heating equipment for a given space.

## Heat Output Basics

All electric heaters are 100% efficient at converting electricity to heat:

- **1 watt = 3.41 BTU/h**
- **1 kW = 3,410 BTU/h**

## Sizing Calculations

### Rule of Thumb

For reasonably insulated spaces in moderate climates:

- **20-30 BTU/h per square foot** for supplemental heat
- **30-50 BTU/h per square foot** for primary heat

### Avalon Mini 3 Coverage

At 2,730 BTU/h, a single Avalon Mini 3 can handle:

| Role | Square Footage |
|------|----------------|
| Primary heat (well-insulated) | 50-90 sq ft |
| Supplemental heat | 90-135 sq ft |
| Background heat boost | 135-200+ sq ft |

### Factors That Affect Sizing

**Increase capacity for:**
- Poor insulation
- Old/leaky windows
- High ceilings
- Cold climate zones
- Rooms with multiple exterior walls

**Decrease capacity for:**
- New construction with modern insulation
- Interior rooms
- Mild climate zones
- Rooms with southern sun exposure

## Use Case Selection

### Kit 1 (Smart Control)
Best when:
- Integrating with existing smart thermostat
- Building custom automations
- Adding to an existing smart home

### Kit 2 (Room Thermostat)
Best when:
- Standalone room heating
- No existing smart thermostat
- "Set and forget" operation desired

### Kit 3 (HVAC Integration)
Best when:
- Whole-home heating desired
- Existing forced-air system
- Two-stage heating strategy

### Kit 4 (Hydronic Integration)
Best when:
- Radiant floor or baseboard heating
- Existing or new hydronic system
- Silent operation required
